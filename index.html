SciProfiles

MDPI • Research Platform • 2023-2024

A networking and collaboration platform for the scientific community, redesigned to serve diverse user needs and achieve 20x growth in monthly active users.

Context & Challenge

Researchers around the world were trying to navigate the platform which felt more like a maze than a resource. MDPI is an open-access publisher that created SciProfiles as an online space for the scientific community to connect, exchange ideas and cooperate. When I joined, the UX/UI was being handled by developers, with occasional input from graphic designers. The goal was to completely revamp SciProfiles - not just to make it more user-friendly but also to demonstrate the value of UX design as a practice.

Discovery & Research

I facilitated all kinds of user research - focus groups, user interviews, card sorting exercises as well as stakeholder interviews. SciProfiles has a highly diverse user base, encompassing seven distinct personas: regular users focused on casual networking, authors searching for co-authors, researchers looking for papers to cite, and editors seeking peer reviewers. The biggest challenge was the messy Information Architecture - one developer joked, "I can't even find a feature I coded last week!" Card sorting exercises with three different groups resulted in a homogeneous information architecture that was vastly different from what we started with.

Design Process

I was the only designer in a project-based team of developers and managers. I started by diving into research activities and running a card sorting exercise. With no clear timeline, I juggled research while laying groundwork for a design system to provide developers with implementable material. Once I had solid understanding of the platform, users, and business needs, I worked with stakeholders to set tactical goals and define a timeline. We adopted design sprints to quickly test ideas and deliver successful ones. This cycle of brainstorming, testing, and iterating kept us busy for almost a year.

Solution & Design

Two major challenges emerged: First, the mobile-first approach was initially rejected due to "survivor bias" - stakeholders claimed mobile wasn't important since few users browsed mobile (because minimum supported width was 768px). We mitigated this by using atomic design principles in our design system and Figma's auto-layout features. After a year-long desktop redesign, mobile and tablet versions were ready in less than a month. Second, information density was crucial for the scientific community's strict display requirements. Through careful ideation and user testing, we found that expert users didn't mind density if it increased usability. We balanced information density with legibility rather than pursuing visual minimalism.

Impact & Results

The redesign was a tremendous success, achieving 20x growth in monthly active users - from 5,000 to 100,000 within one year. The design system was adopted across MDPI's platforms, enhancing development speed and consistency. User satisfaction improved across all seven personas. Key learnings: solid design systems and planning increase delivery speed while lowering complexity; expert users prioritize functionality over visual minimalism; and comprehensive user testing can reveal completely unexpected insights.

Act2Access

Startup • Ad-tech Platform • 2023

A startup solution helping publishers monetize ad-blocked content through surveys and A/B tests, balancing user engagement with revenue generation.

Context & Challenge

Act2Access was a startup aimed at helping publishers with monetization by providing premium content to users who blocked ads. The platform offered alternatives like completing surveys or participating in A/B tests, providing valuable insights to publishers while granting users access to content. The challenge was creating a replacement for internet advertising that would offer users the possibility to view premium content even when using ad blockers, while ensuring publishers could still generate revenue and gather valuable user insights.

Discovery & Research

As the only designer on the project, I worked closely with stakeholders through interviews to understand both the user-facing and back-office systems. I needed to learn about how campaigns were created, run, and reported. All potential users were internal customers, which meant I could reach them anytime for research and testing. This internal access made research and user testing more straightforward than typical projects. I conducted focus groups and user testing with internal users to validate MVP features and refine the user experience.

Design Process

This was a small project with minimal planning involved - we moved straight to execution. After getting acquainted with the product and goals, I quickly drafted the UI elements needed and started ideating both the user-facing surveys and the back-office system. I created UI components and workflows for both users and publishers, designing interactive prototypes that could integrate into various publishing platforms. The process involved rapid iteration with stakeholders to quickly get a first version running, then improving it to a satisfactory level through continuous testing and refinement.

Solution & Design

I designed several types of surveys and A/B tests that would fit into standard ad spaces, along with the backend system for creating campaigns. While it was only a few styles and components - not quite a full "design system" - I established consistent UI patterns before moving to detailed design. The solution balanced user engagement with revenue generation by providing seamless alternatives to traditional advertising. I delivered a scalable framework designed for compatibility across various publishing platforms, ensuring future platform expansion was possible.

Impact & Results

The system was successfully created and tested with positive feedback from publishers. We established a foundation for continued platform growth and provided publishers with actionable insights while maintaining user satisfaction. However, this was ultimately a "proof of concept" experiment that didn't find its way to market. Key learning: even when a project is successfully executed, it doesn't guarantee it will go live. While not the most complex project in terms of scope, it showcased the variety of work and rapid MVP development capabilities in the startup environment.

Belimo Assistant

Belimo • Industrial IoT • 2022-2023

A cross-platform HVAC setup, diagnostics, and management app supporting three OSs, three device types, and seven connection methods with streamlined user experience.

Context & Challenge

Imagine a technician in a service corridor, trying to configure an HVAC valve via smartphone. Belimo Assistant was created to solve these exact challenges - bringing efficiency to professionals worldwide. It replaced three separate apps that were used for the same purpose. By connecting to Belimo devices via cable, internet, Bluetooth, or NFC, the app enables users to configure and monitor performance parameters, diagnose issues, and adjust settings directly from PC, smartphone, or tablet. The main objective was to quickly design and develop a multi-platform app for commissioning and maintenance of Belimo products, as the preceding apps had reached end of life.

Discovery & Research

The primary users were Belimo employees and subcontractors, including HVAC setup, commissioning, and maintenance professionals. They were expert users, accustomed to technical workflows but often limited by environmental challenges like restricted visibility and physical access to devices. While stakeholders provided basic user flows, I supplemented this with real-device testing to identify pain points. Key findings revealed environmental constraints affecting usability, need for rapid iteration due to fragmented tools slowing down prototyping, and interaction differences across wired, Bluetooth, and NFC connections.

Design Process

I started as the sole designer, later joined by two additional UX/UI designers. Initially, I focused on understanding project goals through stakeholder interviews, which proved most insightful despite extensive documentation. Early on, I noticed inefficiencies in fragmented design tools (Adobe XD, Photoshop, Illustrator, Confluence, Zeplin, Jira) and proposed Figma as the single source of truth. Using design tokens and atomic design principles, I migrated the entire system to Figma, leveraging auto layout, nested components, and variables. I simultaneously built a robust design system while brainstorming features and tackling complex connection methods through interactive prototypes.

Solution & Design

I addressed two major challenges: First, tool fragmentation was solved by centralizing everything in Figma, which was adopted across all seven ongoing projects. This enabled faster iterations, leaner communication, and interactive prototypes on real devices. Second, connection complexity across multiple platforms and seven connection methods was addressed through careful error categorization. I designed for "unhappy paths," reducing user-required actions for connection errors from 80% to 20%. For NFC alignment issues, I used the device shape as a visual hint in the top bar and implemented haptic feedback patterns since users often couldn't see the screen while positioning their phone.

Impact & Results

Successfully delivered the project on schedule, merging three apps into one while introducing new connection methods. Achieved 2x faster design process and streamlined iterations with centralized Figma collaboration. Reduced user-facing connection issues by 80% through automated error handling. The Figma-based design system was adopted across all Belimo projects with three software vendors. Key learnings: transitioning design systems to new platforms requires strategic planning but significantly enhances efficiency; real-world testing is invaluable in complex working environments; and environmental usage context can dramatically impact overall usability.

FX Trading Tool

HSBC • Financial Services • 2022

An internal trading tool for HSBC private bankers facilitating complex currency exchanges for high-net-worth clients, designed within strict banking security constraints.

Context & Challenge

HSBC Bank's premium clients have dedicated private bankers who use this tool to execute currency exchanges on behalf of high-ranking clients. These transactions go beyond standard exchanges, involving complex parameters like futures, spot, and forward rates, with options for ultra-high-net-worth clients to purchase bullion. This project presented unique challenges: working in a highly secure banking environment, with limited UI components and no direct access to real users. I included this project because of the demanding constraints that made the UX process both challenging and intriguing.

Discovery & Research

As the only designer on the project, I communicated exclusively with stakeholders who were highly knowledgeable about the product and its usage. Through stakeholder interviews, I gained understanding of users and their working environment, but I also had to learn extensively about complex financial products. I often served as the translator between business requirements and the development team, being the only person with an economics background. The research phase involved understanding both user needs and intricate financial workflows without direct user access.

Design Process

When onboarded, I received a super-secure laptop without internet connection for all work. I approached design in micro increments - presenting yesterday's work in daily meetings while getting assigned next tasks. Despite having two-week sprints, most design work was done daily, solving one small problem after another and incrementally building sketches and later visual design. I used HSBC's internal sketching tool for quick drafts before moving to UI design in Sketch (cloud-based tools like Figma were prohibited). The process required strict adherence to pre-approved UI components and security guidelines.

Solution & Design

Working within HSBC's strict design system, I designed user flows and wireframes for the trading interface using only pre-approved UI components. The solution leveraged HSBC's design system to ensure consistency and compliance while streamlining complex workflows for private bankers managing high-value transactions. I delivered detailed prototypes and comprehensive design documentation for implementation, ensuring every interaction met banking security standards. The constraint of limited UI components actually removed guesswork and improved development speed, though it limited design innovation.

Impact & Results

Successfully delivered the project on time, meeting HSBC's high standards for usability and security. Streamlined workflows for private bankers managing complex high-value transactions involving futures, spot, and forward rates. The app was developed and delivered on schedule, adhering to strict banking guidelines. Key learnings: gained extensive knowledge about banking sector products and development procedures; strict guideline adherence limits innovation but vastly improves development speed by removing guesswork; working under extreme security constraints requires different UX methodologies but can still deliver effective solutions.

HomeRules Poker

Gaming Startup • 2021-2022

An innovative digital poker game replacing physical cards with smartphones for private cards and shared devices for community cards, designed through extensive user research.

Context & Challenge

HomeRules Poker brings players back to the table - literally - by blending digital tools with the social essence of in-person poker. Players use their phones to view private cards, while a shared screen displays community cards, eliminating the need for a dealer or physical poker set. It keeps players' phones busy with the game, so everyone stays focused on playing instead of scrolling social media. The challenge was convincing poker players to adopt a digital alternative without losing the traditional experience, while developing entirely new game mechanics for a unique multi-device setup.

Discovery & Research

This was by far the most research-intensive project I've worked on, with nearly two years of continuous exploration. As part of a six-person team (3 UX, 3 UI designers), I led qualitative research to understand poker players' habits and expectations. Key findings revealed that millennials are the last generation actively playing poker - it's not popular with younger generations. This meant designing for users who aren't as tech-savvy. Players always preferred actions that mimic real poker moves, like hiding screens to peek at cards or "throwing" chips toward the table. Surprisingly, players spent significant amounts on non-functional in-game purchases during interviews about gaming behavior.

Design Process

We approached the project with an iterative process emphasizing collaboration and testing. At each sprint's beginning, we chose specific problems to research, design, or test. I conducted interviews and focus groups with poker players, then worked with the team to brainstorm solutions and refine game mechanics to closely mimic traditional poker. I facilitated communication between designers, developers, and stakeholders, translating business requirements into actionable design tasks. Prototypes were quickly created and tested with players to validate concepts. I organized testing sessions evaluating card viewing, betting, and device connections, using insights to guide interaction improvements.

Solution & Design

I designed natural gestures replicating real-world poker actions and workflows for device pairing, game setup, and in-game actions. The biggest challenge was game setup - connecting multiple devices with aging users and poor TV interfaces. Initial solutions involved typing codes, then QR codes, but both were cumbersome. During interviews, I discovered 60% of users had voice assistants. This sparked developing an Alexa skill that could launch the app and display QR codes instantly, making player joining quick and effortless. The solution integrated voice activation to streamline the traditionally complex setup process while maintaining natural poker-like interactions.

Impact & Results

Successfully solved numerous UX challenges and created a fully operational app running on Android and iOS. Developed completely new game mechanics through extensive user research and testing that poker players found acceptable as alternatives to physical cards and chips. Created natural interaction patterns that preserved the traditional poker experience while leveraging digital advantages. Unfortunately, the product was never launched due to lack of Go-to-Market strategy. Key learnings: mastered comprehensive user research and usability testing methodologies; learned poker gameplay intricacies; developed advanced prototyping skills with complex interactions; and discovered that successful execution doesn't guarantee market launch without proper business strategy.
