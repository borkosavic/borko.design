HomeRules Poker

An innovative digital poker game that displays community cards, betting, and chip balances on a shared TV or tablet, while player cards and actions are shown on their individual devices.

Duration 2017 (2 years)
Skills UX Design
Project Hero Image

Project Overview

An innovative digital poker game that replaces physical cards and chips with smartphones for private cards and a shared device for community cards.

This was by far the most research-intensive project I've ever worked on, with nearly two years of continuous exploration and iteration. Developing completely new game mechanics required extensive user research and testing to ensure poker players would get a better alternative to physical cards or chips.

My Role

  • • Led user research and usability testing to understand player preferences
  • • Designed game mechanics and interactions that mimic traditional poker gestures
  • • Created intuitive workflows for the gameplay

Key Challenges

  • • Convincing poker players to adopt digital alternative without losing traditional experience
  • • Developing entirely new game mechanics for unique multi-device setup
  • • Balancing innovation with familiar poker conventions

Solution

Natural Gesture Design

Designed natural gestures like peeking at cards and swiping chips to mimic physical gameplay, maintaining the tactile experience poker players expect.

Gesture Interaction Flow

Voice-Activated Setup

Integrated Alexa for voice-activated setup, simplifying the game-start process and reducing technical barriers for players.

Voice Setup Interface

Extensive Prototyping

Conducted extensive prototyping and testing to refine mechanics and UX, ensuring the digital experience felt natural and engaging for poker enthusiasts.

Prototype Testing Sessions

Key Findings

Poker Players are Getting Old

The traditional poker demographic is aging, creating an opportunity for more accessible digital solutions.

Keep it Natural

Players strongly prefer interactions that mimic physical poker gestures and behaviors.

Micro-transactions aren't Micro

Traditional poker players are resistant to small, frequent payments typical in digital games.

Results

Product Development Completed

Successfully developed the complete product with innovative multi-device gameplay mechanics and natural gesture interactions that maintained the traditional poker experience while offering digital convenience.

Unfortunately, the product was never released to market, but the extensive research and development process provided valuable insights into digital gaming innovation and user experience design for traditional game formats.
